Instructions

For the Egusi soup:

  1. Season the Meat: Place the beef and stockfish in a pot with sliced onions and seasoning cubes. Add a little water and boil until tender. Set the stock (liquid) aside.
  2. Fry the Base: Heat the palm oil in a large pot for 2 minutes (do not bleach). Add the Iru and the blended pepper mix. Fry on medium heat until the water evaporates and the oil begins to separate at the top.
  3. Form the "Lumps": Season the Egusi paste with a pinch of salt/crayfish. Drop small scoops of the paste into the frying pepper base. Do not stir. Cover the pot and let it steam on low heat for about 10 minutes until the melon clusters become firm.
  4. Simmer: Gently add the cooked meat, dry fish, and the meat stock. Stir very carefully to avoid breaking all the Egusi lumps. Add the ground crayfish. Let it simmer for another 10–15 minutes.
  5. The Green Finish: Stir in the chopped spinach or Ugu. Let it simmer for only 2 minutes so the vegetables stay vibrant. Turn off the heat.

For the Pounded Yam

  1. Boil: Peel and cube the yam. Wash thoroughly and boil in unsalted water until it is very soft (test with a fork; it should slip off easily).
  2. Pound:
    • Traditional: Transfer the hot yam to a mortar and pestle. Pound vigorously until the lumps disappear and the yam becomes a smooth, stretchy dough.
    • Modern: Use a food processor with a dough blade or a heavy-duty stand mixer. Pulse the hot yam cubes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Serve: Mold the yam into a smooth mound while hot and serve alongside the bowl of Egusi.
